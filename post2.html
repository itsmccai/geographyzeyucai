What makes a good map?

To create a map visualizing Airbnb data, start by preparing your data to ensure it includes geographic boundaries (e.g., ZIP codes) and relevant information such as listing prices and counts, with these datasets successfully joined to include necessary fields. Next, symbolize your data by opening the Symbology pane, selecting Unique Values or Graduated Colors, and creating a classification scheme (e.g., for price ranges like 465–1000 and 822–1362 or for count ranges like 10–200 and 627–2048). Assign red and pink to represent areas with above-average price levels, and blue shades for below-average counts, ensuring clear distinctions with contrasting colors. Add labels for ZIP codes by enabling the Label option, customizing the text and size in the Label Class properties. To enhance the map's readability, include a legend, North Arrow, and Scale Bar from the Insert tab, and arrange these elements along with the map in a customized layout.
